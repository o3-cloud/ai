### Condensed transcript

- PMs should move beyond strategy and coding into AI-assisted design: better slides, charts, brainstorming, and executive-ready visual communication.
- Meng To’s current toolkit centers on Codex, Atlas, Whisper Flow, and Obsidian; the key theme is that context is king and local files matter.
- He builds many of his own tools because each workflow is unique, and Codex can generate prep docs, hooks, demos, research, and talking points.
- The workflow uses plugins, skills, and computer use together; computer use is the broadest capability because it can operate across apps.
- Security and guardrails matter, but trusted agents make Gmail, calendar, and support workflows far more practical.
- Skills should be built and updated when the model lacks domain knowledge; examples include design taste, SwiftUI, front-end, and deployment skills.
- The practical build loop starts with plan mode, then screenshots, then iterative prompting, then export to HTML, Figma, or Keynote depending on the final artifact.
- Screenshots and voice input are high-leverage context inputs; multiple agents can work at once, and the PM orchestrates quality.
- Avatars/digital twins are useful for UGC-style presentations and screen-recorded explanations, with appropriate authenticity and disclosure.
- Technical PMs keep their jobs by becoming fluent in tools, models, and workflows; the “last 8%” is human taste and QA.
